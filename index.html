Desert Homesteading

Learning sustainability in the heat

Gardening in Las Vegas can be very rewarding since the hotter climate allows for an extended growing season. However, extreme heat means that you need to be very careful about the way you garden. Here at my garden, we we have a variety of fruits and veggies for this 2023 spring/summer season. Here you can see what I do to live off my land. Learn a few tips and tricks and hopefully have a successful garden of your own!

All the things you can grow

Root Vegetables

Carrots, beets, potatoes, radishes, and turnips, are cool weather crops. Ideal time to plant these crops are August.

Tasty Fruits

Most fruits are warm weather crops. Cantaloupe, strawberries and raspberries love to see spring approaching. Spring doesn't last long in the desert.

Nightshades

Nightshades include tomatoes, peppers, eggplants and potatoes. These plants prefer warm to cool weather. They thrive in the fall here in the desert.

Brasiccas

Brasiccas include broccoli, brussels sprouts, cabbage, cauliflower, collard greens, kale, and turnips. They're all cool weather crops. You have to be very precise to grow these in the desert.

Beneficial Insects

A wolf spider is hanging out on my bell pepper plant. Aside from being a cute little guy, it also helps keep my plant from being eaten. Spiders are a garden's best friend!

Desert Strawberries

I have 3 varieties of strawberries, two are everbearing, one is June bearing. Everbearing gives two crops a year. Usually one in June and another in September.
